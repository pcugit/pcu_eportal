# PCU Admission Portal Feature Documentation

This document describes the major features of the PCU Admission Portal according to each portal, dashboard, and user role.

## Admission Portal

The Admission Portal is the main entry point for prospective students who want to apply to Precious Cornerstone University. It allows users to explore available programmes, purchase application forms, submit their admission information, upload documents, make payments, and track the progress of their applications.

Key features:

- Users can create applicant accounts and log in securely.
- Applicants can purchase application forms for different programme categories, including undergraduate, postgraduate, part-time, HND conversion, foundation/JUPEB, IJMB, UTME, and direct entry programmes.
- Applicants can select their preferred programme and complete the correct application form based on the admission category.
- Applicants can save application information and continue later.
- Applicants can submit completed applications for review.
- Applicants can upload required supporting documents such as certificates, passport photographs, transcripts, and other admission documents.
- Uploaded documents can be checked for quality before submission.
- Applicants can view their application status from the dashboard.
- Applicants can pay application fees, acceptance fees, tuition fees, and other applicable fees.
- Applicants can view their payment history and download payment receipts.
- Applicants who are offered admission can view and print admission letters.
- Applicants can download admission-related documents such as medical forms, admission notices, and affidavit forms.
- Applicants can respond to alternative course recommendations from the admission office.
- Applicants can change their passwords and manage basic account access.

## Applicant Dashboard

The Applicant Dashboard is the main workspace for applicants after they log in. It gives applicants a single place to manage applications, payments, documents, and admission outcomes.

Key features:

- Applicants can view all applications linked to their account.
- Applicants can see whether an application is pending, submitted, reviewed, approved, rejected, or admitted.
- Applicants can continue an unfinished form or view a submitted form.
- Applicants can upload, replace, delete, or download documents where allowed.
- Applicants can submit completed applications for admission review.
- Applicants can see payment requirements and make the required payments.
- Applicants can verify payment status after returning from the payment gateway.
- Applicants can download payment receipts.
- Applicants can view admission decisions once released.
- Admitted applicants can print admission letters.
- Admitted applicants can download medical examination forms, admission notices, and affidavit forms.
- Applicants can receive and respond to recommended alternative courses.
- Applicants can accept or decline recommended programmes.

## Staff Portal

The Staff Portal is the shared login area for university staff who manage admissions, applications, academic records, results, students, and system settings. Staff users are redirected to the correct dashboard based on their assigned role.

Key features:

- Staff can log in securely with staff credentials.
- Staff users are routed to dashboards based on their roles.
- Supported staff roles include admission officer, postgraduate admin, postgraduate dean, part-time admin, ICT/admin, lecturer, DEO, HOD, dean, and registrar.
- Staff can change default or existing passwords.
- Staff sessions are protected by authentication and automatic timeout.

## Admission Officer Dashboard

The Admission Officer Dashboard is used by admissions staff to manage regular admission applications and admission letters.

Key features:

- The admission officer can view dashboard statistics for applications and admission activity.
- The admission officer can view lists of applications by status.
- The admission officer can open a full applicant profile and review submitted information.
- The admission officer can review uploaded documents.
- The admission officer can approve or reject applications.
- The admission officer can recommend alternative courses to applicants.
- The admission officer can revoke admission where necessary.
- The admission officer can preview admission letters before sending them.
- The admission officer can send admission letters to individual applicants.
- The admission officer can send admission letters in batches.
- The admission officer can send letters by faculty or department.
- The admission officer can view sent and failed admission letter records.
- The admission officer can resend failed admission letters.
- The admission officer can manage programme-related admission settings such as fees, deadlines, and locks.
- The admission officer can view admitted students and course registration details where available.

## Postgraduate Admin Dashboard

The Postgraduate Admin Dashboard is used to manage postgraduate applications, evaluations, admission decisions, and postgraduate admission letters.

Key features:

- PG admin can view postgraduate application statistics.
- PG admin can view and filter postgraduate applications.
- PG admin can open detailed postgraduate application records.
- PG admin can review applicant biodata, academic history, programme selection, documents, and passport photograph.
- PG admin can print or download postgraduate application forms.
- PG admin can complete or review postgraduate evaluation records.
- PG admin can approve, reject, or process postgraduate applications.
- PG admin can send postgraduate admission letters to individual applicants.
- PG admin can preview postgraduate admission letters before sending.
- PG admin can send postgraduate admission letters by department.
- PG admin can filter applicants by department and degree type where applicable.
- PG admin can view sent and failed postgraduate admission letters.
- PG admin can resend failed postgraduate admission letters.

## Postgraduate Dean Dashboard

The Postgraduate Dean Dashboard is designed for academic review of postgraduate applications.

Key features:

- The PG dean can view postgraduate application statistics.
- The PG dean can view postgraduate applications awaiting academic attention.
- The PG dean can open detailed postgraduate application records.
- The PG dean can review applicant documents and academic qualifications.
- The PG dean can print postgraduate application forms.
- The PG dean can complete or update academic evaluation information.
- The PG dean can support the postgraduate application decision process through evaluation and recommendations.

## Part-Time Admin Dashboard

The Part-Time Admin Dashboard is used to manage part-time and HND-related applications.

Key features:

- PT admin can view part-time application statistics.
- PT admin can view and filter part-time applications.
- PT admin can open full part-time application details.
- PT admin can review applicant information and uploaded documents.
- PT admin can download applicant documents.
- PT admin can approve, reject, or process part-time applications.
- PT admin can print part-time application forms.
- PT admin can preview part-time admission letters.
- PT admin can send part-time admission letters by department.
- PT admin can view sent and failed letter records.
- PT admin can resend failed part-time admission letters.

## Student Portal

The Student Portal is used by admitted students after they have been converted from applicants into students. It supports academic registration, payments, documents, and student profile access.

Key features:

- Students can log in with student credentials.
- Students can view profile details such as name, matric number, programme, level, session, and email.
- Students can change their default password on first login.
- Students can register courses for the active academic session and semester.
- Students can view available, compulsory, and registered courses.
- Students can search for courses during registration.
- Students can submit course registration.
- Students can view course registration history.
- Students can print course registration forms.
- Students can view tuition fee information.
- Students can make school fee payments.
- Students can view payment history.
- Students can download payment receipts.
- Students can download admission letters and other admission-related documents where available.

## Postgraduate Student Portal

The Postgraduate Student Portal is for admitted postgraduate students.

Key features:

- PG students can log in through the postgraduate student login page.
- PG students can view postgraduate profile information, including matric number, programme, session, and email.
- PG students must change default passwords on first login.
- PG students can access course registration.
- PG students can print registered course forms.
- PG students can pay school fees.
- PG students can view payment history.
- PG students can download successful payment receipts.
- PG students can access admission-related document options.
- PG students can update their passwords.

## Part-Time Student Portal

The Part-Time Student Portal is for admitted part-time students.

Key features:

- Part-time students can log in through the part-time student login page.
- Part-time students can view profile information, including matric number, programme, level, session, and email.
- Part-time students must change default passwords on first login.
- Part-time students can register courses.
- Part-time students can print course registration forms.
- Part-time students can pay school fees.
- Part-time students can view payment records and receipts.
- Part-time students can access admission-related document options.

## ICT Dashboard

The ICT Dashboard is used by ICT/admin users to manage system settings, users, students, staff, and result processing tools.

Key features:

- ICT users can view system status and pending result information.
- ICT users can manage global portal settings.
- ICT users can set or update the current academic session.
- ICT users can set or update the current semester.
- ICT users can lock or unlock result upload.
- ICT users can manage course registration-related settings.
- ICT users can manage active semester settings.
- ICT users can search and update student records.
- ICT users can create staff accounts.
- ICT users can view and filter staff accounts.
- ICT users can update staff roles, departments, faculties, titles, staff IDs, and account status.
- ICT users can access the result processor.
- ICT users can process, save, and manage pending result records.

## Lecturer Dashboard

The Lecturer Dashboard is used by lecturers to manage assigned courses and submit student scores.

Key features:

- Lecturers can view assigned courses.
- Lecturers can view students registered for their assigned courses.
- Lecturers can enter continuous assessment and examination scores.
- The system can compute grades from entered scores.
- Lecturers can save pending result work.
- Lecturers can submit scores for approval.
- Lecturers can view pending submitted results.
- Result upload access can be controlled by ICT settings.

## DEO Dashboard

The DEO Dashboard supports data entry officers who perform score-entry duties similar to lecturers.

Key features:

- DEO users are directed to the lecturer-style score entry workspace.
- DEO users can enter and manage scores where permitted.
- DEO users can submit results for review according to the result workflow.

## HOD Dashboard

The HOD Dashboard is used by heads of department to manage department courses, staff assignments, and result approvals.

Key features:

- HODs can view department-level dashboard statistics.
- HODs can view department courses.
- HODs can create department courses.
- HODs can view department staff.
- HODs can assign courses to lecturers or staff.
- HODs can remove course assignments.
- HODs can view submitted results from the department.
- HODs can approve submitted scores.
- HODs can reject submitted scores with reasons.
- HODs can approve scores in bulk.
- HODs can monitor pending result records.

## Dean Dashboard

The Dean Dashboard is used for faculty-level academic oversight.

Key features:

- Deans can view faculty-level academic statistics.
- Deans can review faculty result records.
- Deans can filter results by status, session, and semester.
- Deans can view GPA summaries.
- Deans can monitor academic performance and result processing at faculty level.

## Registrar Dashboard

The Registrar Dashboard is used by the registrar's office to manage student records and transcript workflows.

Key features:

- Registrar users can view registrar dashboard statistics.
- Registrar users can search student records.
- Registrar users can view student transcript information.
- Registrar users can create transcript request records.
- Registrar users can view transcript request history.
- Registrar users can sign transcripts.
- Registrar users can issue transcripts.
- Registrar users can track transcript processing status.

## Payment Features

The portal includes a centralized payment system used by applicants and students.

Key features:

- Users can initiate application fee payments.
- Users can initiate acceptance fee payments.
- Users can initiate tuition or school fee payments.
- Users can select installment options where applicable.
- Payments are processed through Interswitch.
- The system verifies payment status after payment.
- Pending payments can be rechecked automatically.
- Successful payments generate receipt numbers.
- Users can download payment receipts.
- Payment status can trigger downstream actions such as application activation, student conversion, or matric number generation.

## Document and Letter Features

The portal supports document uploads, document downloads, application printing, admission letters, and other PDF documents.

Key features:

- Applicants can upload required admission documents.
- The system validates supported document types.
- Uploaded images can be compressed.
- Uploaded documents can be scanned for quality issues.
- Staff can review applicant documents.
- Staff can download applicant documents.
- Applicants and students can download generated forms and letters.
- Admission letters can be previewed before sending.
- Admission letters can be sent individually or in batches.
- Failed admission letters can be resent.
- Application forms can be printed for PG and PT applicants.
- Medical forms, admission notices, affidavit forms, and payment receipts can be generated or downloaded.

## Result and Course Registration Features

The portal supports student course registration and academic result workflows.

Key features:

- Students can view available courses for their programme and level.
- Students can register courses by semester.
- Students can print course registration forms.
- Lecturers and DEOs can enter scores.
- HODs can approve or reject scores.
- Deans can review faculty results.
- Registrars can use approved academic records for transcript workflows.
- ICT can control result upload and active academic settings.
